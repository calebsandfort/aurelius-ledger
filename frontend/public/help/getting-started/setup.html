Setup Guide ​

This guide walks you through creating your account and logging your first trade.

Creating Your Account ​

Step 1: Open the app in your browser. You will see a sign-in page with options to sign in or create an account.

Step 2: Click "Create Account" or the sign-up link.

Step 3: Enter your email and choose a password. That's it — no verification steps or extra info needed.

Step 4: After creating your account, you are automatically signed in and taken to your dashboard.

What Happens to Your Data

Your data is stored securely and associated with your account. It is only used to power your dashboard and insights. The AI does not share your trading data with anyone.

Your First Look at the Dashboard ​

When you first log in, the dashboard is empty. You will see:

  • A header with your name and a "Sign Out" button
  • A large P&L chart — currently showing a placeholder because you have no trades yet
  • Two smaller charts — Discipline Score and Agency Score, also empty
  • An Insights panel — prompting you to log trades to unlock insights
  • A text box at the bottom — this is where you log trades

The dashboard is designed so you can see everything at a glance. No clicking around to check your session.

Logging Your First Trade ​

Step 1: Look at the bottom of your screen. You will see a text input that says something like:

"Describe your trade (e.g., 'Long NQ at 17800, exited at 17850 for +$500')"

Step 2: Type what happened in plain English. For example:

"Longed NQ at 17500 on the pullback, hit my target at 17600 for +$500. Waited patiently for the setup."

Or keep it simple:

"Won $300 on ES, followed my plan"

Or honest:

"FOMO'd into a short, knew it was wrong, lost $250"

Step 3: Press Enter or click "Log Trade."

What happens next:

  • The input flashes green to confirm your trade was logged
  • The text box clears and refocuses automatically
  • Your trade appears in the charts above
  • The AI reads your description and figures out the details

What the AI Extracts ​

When you type a trade description, the AI pulls out:

  • Direction — Long or short
  • Outcome — Win, loss, or breakeven
  • P&L — The dollar amount (or a rough estimate like "small winner" or "nice loss")
  • Discipline score — Based on how you describe the trade (patience, following rules, or chasing)
  • Agency score — Based on whether you sound in control or reactive

You do not need to be precise. The system handles approximations like "small winner" or "nice loss."

Do Not Overthink It

You do not need any special format. All of these work:

  • "Long NQ, made $500"
  • "Short ES, lost $200, chased the entry"
  • "Scratched the trade, no harm done"
  • "Revenge traded after the loser, -$400"

After Your First Trade ​

Once you log your first trade:

  • The P&L chart shows a single point — your cumulative P&L so far
  • The Discipline and Agency charts show your first score
  • The session stats appear in the Insights panel

Keep logging trades throughout your session. The more data, the more useful the charts become.

What "Session" Means ​

A session runs from when you start logging trades until you stop for more than 30 minutes. After that idle time, any new trades start a fresh session.

The system tracks each session separately, so you can look back at past days and see how your sessions went.

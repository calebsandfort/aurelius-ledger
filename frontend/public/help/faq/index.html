Frequently Asked Questions ​

Answers to common questions about using Aurelius Ledger.

Getting Started ​

What should I type when logging a trade? ​

Anything that describes what happened. Be honest and specific. Examples:

  • "Longed NQ at 17500, made $500"
  • "Short ES, lost $200, chased the entry"
  • "Waited for the setup, exited at target, +$300"
  • "FOMO'd into the breakout, knew it was wrong, -$150"

The more context you give, the better the AI can understand your trade.

What if I make a typo? ​

Just resubmit. The system processes each entry independently. If you made a mistake, type the correct version and submit again.

Can I edit a trade after logging it? ​

Not in the current version. If you need to correct something, log a new trade with the corrected info, or contact support for help.

How long is a "session"? ​

A session runs from when you start logging trades until you stop for more than 30 minutes. After 30 minutes of inactivity, any new trade starts a fresh session.

Each session is tracked separately, so you can see how different trading days went.

Understanding Your Data ​

What do the discipline and agency scores mean? ​

Discipline — Did you follow your rules?

  • +1: You waited for your setup, followed your plan
  • 0: Neutral
  • -1: You deviated — chased, overtraded, ignored rules

Agency — Are you in control?

  • +1: You made an intentional decision
  • 0: Neutral
  • -1: You reacted emotionally — revenge trading, FOMO, fear

Why did I get a -1 discipline score? ​

Common triggers include:

  • Using words like "chased," "FOMO," "revenge," "too early," "knew it was wrong"
  • Taking a trade that does not match your stated setup
  • Entering without waiting for your confirmation signal
  • Increasing size after a loss

The AI looks at the words you use and the context of your description.

What does "insufficient signal" mean? ​

That means the AI could not confidently determine your score based on what you typed. Your description was too vague.

To get a more accurate score, be more descriptive:

  • Instead of "won on ES," say "waited for the pullback to the 9 EMA, entered long, hit target for +$400"

How is tilt detected? ​

The system watches for patterns in your discipline and agency scores. If you get 3 consecutive -1 scores (either discipline or agency), you will see an amber warning. At 4+, it turns orange.

The warning is triggered by patterns, not by individual trades. A single bad trade will not trigger it.

What does the warning bar mean? ​

  • Amber (yellow): 3 consecutive discipline or agency -1 scores. You might be slipping.
  • Orange: 4+ consecutive discipline or agency -1 scores. You are likely tilted — consider stepping away.

The warning is not a punishment. It is a signal to pay attention to what is happening.

Privacy and Data ​

Is my data private? ​

Yes. Your trading data is stored securely and associated with your account. It is used only to power your dashboard and insights.

The AI does not share your data with anyone. Your trades are not visible to other users.

Does the AI remember my past sessions? ​

No. Each session is independent. The AI does not carry over information from one day to the next. It only looks at your current session data to generate insights.

Can I delete my data? ​

Yes. Contact support to request data deletion. You can also export your data if you want to keep a copy.

What data do you collect? ​

We collect what you type (trade descriptions), the extracted trade details (direction, P&L, scores), and session timestamps. We do not collect personal information beyond your email.

Troubleshooting ​

The dashboard is not loading ​

Try refreshing the page. If the problem persists, check your internet connection or try clearing your browser cache.

My trade did not go through ​

Make sure you pressed Enter or clicked "Log Trade." You should see a green flash on the input if it succeeded.

If it still does not work, try logging the trade again. If the problem continues, contact support.

The charts are not updating ​

The charts update after each trade. If they are not updating:

  • Refresh the page
  • Make sure JavaScript is enabled in your browser
  • Check your internet connection

I disagree with a score — what can I do? ​

Scores are based on your description. If you disagree, try describing trades more explicitly in the future. The score is a starting point for reflection, not a definitive judgment.

Tips and Best Practices ​

When is the best time to log a trade? ​

Log right after you close a trade, while the details are fresh. The trade entry box is always at the bottom of your screen, so it is easy to do between trades.

How do I get better insights? ​

Log more trades. Insights unlock after 5 trades and get richer as you log more. Try to describe your trades with context — what setup you were looking for, how you felt, whether you followed your plan.

Should I log every trade? ​

Yes, for the best results. Even scratched trades and small losers. The more complete your data, the more useful the insights.

What if I trade multiple instruments? ​

The system does not differentiate between instruments in the main dashboard. Log your trades as usual — the system looks at patterns in your behavior, not which contract you traded.

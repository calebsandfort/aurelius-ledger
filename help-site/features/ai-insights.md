# AI Insights

The AI Insights panel is like having a trading coach watching your session and offering observations. It is not judgment — it is pattern recognition designed to help you stay aware.

## When Insights Appear

Insights unlock after you log 5 trades in a session. Before that, you see basic stats but no AI observations.

The more trades you log, the more useful the insights become. With 5-10 trades, the AI can start seeing patterns. With more, it can offer richer observations.

## What Insights Look Like

Each insight appears as a card in the Insights panel. They are short — 1 or 2 sentences max — and action-oriented.

**Example insights:**

- "Your discipline improved after the second loss. You waited for setups instead of chasing."
- "Three of your four losses came after winning trades. Watch for revenge trading patterns."
- "Your patience paid off on the long ES trade — the wait led to your biggest winner."
- "Watch the agency dip in trades 4-6. You may be reacting to the market rather than planning."

## Insight Categories

Insights fall into a few categories:

### Positive Reinforcement
Green-colored insights that highlight good patterns:

- "You stayed disciplined after a loss — that is tough to do."
- "Your best trade came after waiting for your setup. The patience worked."

### Caution Insights
Yellow-colored insights that point out patterns to watch:

- "Your discipline score dropped after the third trade."
- "You have taken three consecutive losses. Consider taking a break."

### Warning Insights
Orange or red-colored insights for serious patterns:

- "Multiple revenge trade signals detected."
- "Your agency scores suggest emotional trading in the last few trades."

## How Insights Are Generated

The AI reads your recent trade descriptions and scores, looking for patterns:

- Consecutive wins or losses
- Score trends (are you getting more or less disciplined?)
- Timing patterns (are certain trades happening at specific times?)
- Word choice (do you describe trades as "chased," "FOMO," "revenge"?)

It then generates a short, actionable observation.

You see up to 3 insights at a time. As you log more trades, older insights may be replaced by newer, more relevant ones.

## What to Do With Insights

Read them, think about them, and use them however makes sense.

If the AI notices you chase after losses, that is useful information — now you can be more aware of it. If it points out that your best trades come after waiting, that reinforces the behavior you want.

The goal is awareness, not perfection.

:::tip Insights Are Suggestions
The AI is not always right. It sees patterns in your data, but it does not know what is happening in your head. Take insights as prompts for reflection, not as definitive judgments.
:::

## Insight Severity Colors

| Color | Meaning |
|-------|--------|
| Green | Positive pattern detected — keep it up |
| Yellow | Something to watch — stay aware |
| Gray | Neutral observation |

The colors help you scan the insights quickly and prioritize what to think about.

## Last Updated Timestamp

Each insight panel shows when the insights were last generated. This helps you know how current the observations are.

Insights regenerate after each new trade you log, so they always reflect your most recent session data.

## Why Only 3 Insights?

The dashboard shows a maximum of 3 insights at a time. This keeps things scannable. You can see the most important patterns without being overwhelmed.

If you want to explore more, you can log more trades and see new insights appear.

## Examples of What the AI Might Notice

Here are some realistic examples of patterns the AI might detect:

**"I noticed you used the word 'chased' in three of your last five trades."**
→ This could trigger a caution insight about overtrading.

**"Your win rate is 60%, but your average loss is 2x your average win."**
→ This might trigger an insight about risk management.

**"After each loss, your next trade discipline score drops."**
→ This could trigger a revenge trading warning.

**"You described two trades as 'waited patiently' — and both were wins."**
→ This could trigger a positive reinforcement insight about patience.

## Working With the Coach

Think of the AI coach as a trading buddy who is paying attention. It is not there to lecture you. It is there to notice things you might miss in the moment.

Use the insights to:

- Catch patterns you would not otherwise see
- Reinforce good habits
- Get an outside perspective on your session

And remember — the system is not judging you. It is just showing you what is happening so you can decide what to do next.
